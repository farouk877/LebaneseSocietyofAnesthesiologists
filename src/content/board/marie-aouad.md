Dr. Marie Aouad is Chairperson of the Department of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine at AUBMC. She is a distinguished anesthesiologist with expertise in pediatric and pediatric cardiac anesthesiology, perioperative medicine, and obstetric anesthesia.

She earned her MD from Saint Joseph University in Beirut and completed residency training at Hopital Hotel-Dieu-Lyon, affiliated with Universite Claude Bernard Lyon in France, and at AUBMC. She holds certifications from the Arab Board of Health Specializations and the U.S. National Board of Echocardiography.

Dr. Aouad has authored more than 120 publications and contributes to the field through research, editorial work, and invited lectures. Her scholarship spans clinical studies, systematic reviews, data-driven analyses, and healthcare leadership, with a focus on improving outcomes in high-risk patient populations and advancing the specialty within complex healthcare contexts.

She has been invited to speak at numerous national and international conferences, most recently at the 19th World Congress of Anaesthesiologists in April 2026, highlighting Lebanon's presence on the global stage. She serves as a reviewer and editorial board member for leading anesthesiology journals and is an active member of the Arab Board of Anesthesiology and the Pediatric Anesthesia Committee of the World Federation of Societies of Anesthesiologists.

Since assuming the role of Chairperson in September 2019, Dr. Aouad has advanced the department across its clinical, academic, and research missions despite challenging institutional and national contexts. Under her leadership, AUBMC became the first institution outside the United States to join the Multicenter Perioperative Outcomes Group. She also established a real-time perioperative dashboard linked to MPOG, implemented an Epic-based digital resource platform, led the residency program to ACGME-I accreditation, introduced simulation-based training and faculty development initiatives, and established a departmental research unit to strengthen scholarship and academic productivity.
